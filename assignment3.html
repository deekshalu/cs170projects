A Brief History of Genentech

Genentech was founded on April 7th, 1976 and became the first biotechnology company to go public. They started very humbly, having no assets or necessary equipment. Still, they were able use recombinant E. coli bacteria to produce the human protein somatostanin. This same process was later used by the company to produce the human horman insulin, a very huge breakthrough for the company and the world of medicine. According to a European Biotech website, previous to this research, animal versions of the insulin was harvested. The National Human Genome Research Institute reports that the company isolated genes for human insulin and growth hormone to create the first recombinant DNA drug, human insulin. The company developed this drug as a solution to the problems the previous method had. When using animal pancreas to harvest the insulin hormone, one company. Eli Lily had to use 56 million animals per year to meet insulin demands. Insulin is very important for diabetics since they lack the hormone, and insuline hormone reguales the body's blood sugar levels. The process of the experiment and their research are summarized on their website.

The development of recombinant insulin changed the face of biotechnology and pharameuticals. Many years later, Genentech is often considered the founder of the industry. In 2009, the company became a member of the Roche Group. Based in Switzerland, Roche develops innovative medications and is considered to be a pioneer in healthcare. According to The New York Times, prior to Genentech's acquisition, it was considered to be one of the oldest and most successful of all biotechnology companies. The merger has shown that both Roche and Genentech have closer ties than most acquisition merger do, with Roche's three big drugs having been developed by Genentech. From being one of the world's first and biggest biotech companies, Genentech has made history both in the past and in the present with is acquisition by Roche.
